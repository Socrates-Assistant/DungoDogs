Engineering, Foundation & Slab Warning

△ WARNING: CRITICAL GROUND STABILITY & FOUNDATION REQUIREMENTS

  • NO CONCRETE SLAB REQUIRED: This structure includes an integrated, fully engineered raised flooring system and does not require a poured concrete slab.
  • MANDATORY SURFACE PREPARATION: The structure must NOT be placed directly on raw earth, grass, or uncompacted ground. It must be installed on a level, highly compacted, and well-draining base (e.g., compacted road base, crusher dust, or pre-leveled concrete pavers/piles at load-bearing points).
  • WEIGHT STABILITY HAZARD: Due to the heavy-duty 1,100kg unladen weight of this structure, any ground sinking or uneven settling will cause structural shifting. This can lead to panel warping, frame twisting, and door/window misalignment.
  • WIND SECUREMENT & ANCHORING: While the 1,100kg weight offers substantial ballast against wind uplift, this structure must still be mechanically anchored to the ground. The purchaser must utilise heavy-duty earth augers, ground screw anchors, or deep-set pegs capable of resisting local wind forces.
  • EXCLUSION OF LIABILITY: The supplier accepts zero liability for structural failure, operational issues, or property damage resulting from poor ground preparation, soil erosion, shifting foundations, or a failure to install appropriate ground anchors.

Wind Rating & Tie-Down Warning

Recommended Tie-Down & Anchoring Options

  • ENGINEERING COMPLIANCE: While the 1,100kg unladen weight of this garden shed provides substantial built-in ballast, it is highly recommended to install mechanical ground anchors. This ensures safety in high-wind events and maintains structural alignment.
  • APPROVED SPECIFICATION SYSTEM: For sites utilising a compacted road base, crusher dust, or soil foundation instead of a concrete slab, the supplier recommends using a certified percussion earth anchor system, such as the AncorLoc Duckbill anchor range (or an equivalent system engineered for portable and Class 10a buildings).
  • SITE AND WIND CODE ADAPTABILITY: Wind forces and soil types vary drastically across Australia. The purchaser should consult an anchor specialist or structural engineer to determine the exact anchor model, depth, and pull-out rating required for their local wind classification (e.g., N1, N2, N3) and soil density.
  • INSTALLATION METHOD: Tie-down anchor cables or tensioning straps must be structurally fixed to the main load-bearing perimeter beams of the shed’s steel chassis. Do not attach anchors to non-structural wall paneling or flashings.
  • LIMITATION OF LIABILITY: The supplier does not manufacture, test, or supply anchoring hardware. The purchaser assumes total liability for sourcing, engineering, and installing a tie-down system adequate for their specific site conditions. The supplier accepts zero liability for wind-uplift damage or ground sliding resulting from a failure to anchor the building correctly.
